Kurt Cobain

The man who changed the world

Here's a time line of Kurt Cobain's life:

  • February 20, 1967: Kurt Cobain is born in Aberdeen, Washington, U.S. His generally happy childhood takes a turn when his parents divorce when he is nine years old.

  • December 1, 1985: Kurt moves in with Jesse Read, marking a pivotal moment in his life.

  • 1987: Cobain and bassist Krist Novoselic form the grunge band Nirvana. They recruit various drummers and begin recording demo tapes and playing small shows in the Northwest.

  • 1988: Nirvana signs with the Seattle independent record label Sub Pop and releases their first single, “Love Buzz.”

  • 1989: Nirvana's debut album, “Bleach,” is released.

  • 1990:Drummer Dave Grohl joins Nirvana.

  • 1991: Nirvana releases their major-label debut album, “Nevermind,” featuring the iconic hit single “Smells Like Teen Spirit.”

  • April 5, 1994: Tragically, Kurt Cobain dies in Seattle, Washington, at the age of 27, leaving behind a lasting legacy in the world of rock music.

    • Kurt Cobain's impact on music and culture remains profound, and his memory endures through his powerful lyrics and contributions to the grunge movement.
